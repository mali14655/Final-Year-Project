Interviewer: Ahmed, thanks for your time. How often do you order food online?

Interviewee: Almost daily. I'm a student, so I don't cook much. Budget is my main concern.

Interviewer: What problems do you run into?

Interviewee: Hidden fees. The price on the menu is not the price at checkout. Delivery fee, service fee, surge fee — I abandon the cart when the total jumps by 30 percent.

Interviewer: Same as others we've talked to — checkout issues?

Interviewee: Yes, exactly. Slow checkout too. Too many screens. Also when the restaurant is out of stock, I only find out after I've paid. That happened twice last month.

Interviewer: What features would you want?

Interviewee: Split payment with friends — we order together in the dorm. And better filters: halal, under 300 rupees, student discounts.

Interviewer: How do you feel about the apps overall?

Interviewee: Mixed. Convenient but stressful. I feel like they're designed to make me spend more, not to help me find cheap good food quickly.

Interviewer: Thanks Ahmed, this is very useful.

Interviewee: Anytime.
